The retail sales dataset was analyzed to understand customer behavior, product performance, and revenue trends.

Key Findings

1. Revenue Performance
Total revenue was generated from multiple sales transactions.
A small number of customers contributed a significant portion of overall revenue.

2. Top Customers
Top customers generated the highest sales value.
Repeat customers contributed more revenue than one-time customers.

3. Product Performance
A few products accounted for most sales.
Best-selling products consistently appeared in high-value transactions.

4. Sales Trends
Revenue fluctuated across different periods.
Certain months showed stronger sales performance than others.

5. High-Value Transactions
Several transactions contributed significantly to overall revenue.
These transactions can be analyzed further to identify premium customers.

6. Customer Behavior
Returning customers generally had higher average purchase values.
Customer retention can positively impact overall revenue.


The analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to transform raw 
sales data into actionable business insights. These findings 
can help improve decision-making related to customers, 
products, and revenue growth.
